Do paid doctors prescribe more? — top 12 drugs by industry payments, 2024

Avg Medicare Part D claims per prescriber, paid vs unpaid for that exact drug. Public CMS data (Open Payments × Part D).

Across all 12 of the most-paid drugs, prescribers who took money wrote more — from +1.6% to +57% — and the gap survives within-specialty adjustment. The control, Metformin (generic, $0 in payments by design), has no paid group at all.

“Within-specialty lift” = each specialty’s paid-vs-unpaid gap, weighted by its paid prescribers, counting only specialties with n≥30 on both sides — so it can’t be explained by specialty mix. All cells above have n≥30. The $10k+ payment tail (not shown) is typically <10 prescribers per drug.

Correlation, not causation. This is observational public data. Manufacturers also target physicians who already prescribe more, and even within specialty residual confounding (patient volume, subspecialty, academic setting) can remain — so a higher paid-group average cannot be read as payments causing prescribing. Part D suppresses any prescriber-drug row with fewer than 11 claims.
